At the Restaurant

In this lesson you will learn useful expressions for ordering food, asking for the menu, speaking with the waiter and paying the bill in Brazilian Portuguese.

💡 Useful Expressions

When ordering food in Brazil, these expressions are very common:

Eu gostaria de... (I would like...)

Eu quero... (I want...)

O cardápio, por favor. (The menu, please.)

A conta, por favor. (The bill, please.)

🍽️ Restaurant Dialogue

Garçom: Boa noite! Bem-vindo ao restaurante.
(Good evening! Welcome to the restaurant.)

Cliente: Boa noite! O cardápio, por favor.
(Good evening! The menu, please.)

Garçom: Claro! O que você gostaria de beber?
(Of course! What would you like to drink?)

Cliente: Eu gostaria de um suco de laranja, por favor.
(I would like an orange juice, please.)

Garçom: E para comer?
(And to eat?)

Cliente: Eu quero frango com arroz e feijão.
(I want chicken with rice and beans.)

Garçom: Perfeito! Mais alguma coisa?
(Perfect! Anything else?)

Cliente: Não, obrigado.
(No, thank you.)

🇧🇷 Brazilian Culture

In Brazil, lunch is usually the biggest meal of the day. Many restaurants offer a prato feito (PF), a traditional meal that often includes rice, beans, meat, salad and fries. Tipping is optional because many restaurants already include a 10% service charge on the bill.

🎧 Listening Practice

Repeat these expressions aloud:

  • O cardápio, por favor.
  • Eu gostaria de um café.
  • A conta, por favor.
  • Obrigado pela comida.
