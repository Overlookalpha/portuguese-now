💡 Did You Know?

Even though the alphabet is the same, pronunciation is one of the biggest differences between English and Brazilian Portuguese. Mastering the alphabet first will make reading and speaking much easier throughout this course.

🔥 Difficult Letters

Some Brazilian Portuguese letters are pronounced very differently from English. Here are the ones students usually find the most challenging.

🇧🇷 R (Erre)

At the beginning of a word, R is usually pronounced from the throat, similar to the English H.

Example: Rua (Street)

🇧🇷 J (Jota)

The letter J sounds like the s in the English word measure.

Example: Janela (Window)

🇧🇷 X (Xis)

The letter X can have different sounds depending on the word.

Example: Xícara (Cup)

🇧🇷 H (Agá)

The letter H is normally silent in Brazilian Portuguese.

Example: Hotel

🇧🇷 G (Gê)

Before E or I, the letter G sounds similar to the English sound in measure.

Example: Gelo (Ice)
